<p><p>Real-time PCR machines employ different types of light sources for monitoring amplification in PCR processes. LED-based systems are efficient, providing quick heat-up times and longer lifespans, making them energy-efficient. Halogen lamp systems offer high-intensity light for better detection sensitivity but may have shorter lifespans and higher energy consumption. Other types include xenon lamps and laser-based systems, which cater to specific research needs. The choice of technology impacts performance, operational costs, and application suitability in molecular biology.</p></p>
