<p><p>The non-woven fabrics market comprises various types, including spunbond, spunlace, needle punch, meltblown, and wet laid fabrics. Spunbond non-woven fabrics are characterized by durable, lightweight layers, ideal for agriculture and hygiene products. Spunlace non-wovens combine fibers using high-pressure water jets, making them soft and absorbent, often used in wipes. Needle punch fabrics are created by mechanically entangling fibers, suitable for carpets and insulation. Meltblown fabrics are known for their fine filtration properties, essential for medical applications. Wet laid non-wovens utilize water suspension for a uniform structure, often used in industrial applications. Other types include composites and bio-based non-wovens, catering to diverse industries.</p></p>
