<p><p>Radio Detection and Ranging (RADAR) is a technology that uses radio waves to detect and locate objects, measuring their distance and speed. The RADAR market is primarily segmented into two types: Pulsed Radar and Continuous Wave (CW) Radar. Pulsed Radar transmits short bursts of radio waves, allowing for precise distance measurements, often used in aviation and weather monitoring. In contrast, Continuous Wave Radar emits a constant signal and is effective for speed detection, commonly employed in traffic enforcement and automotive applications.</p></p>
